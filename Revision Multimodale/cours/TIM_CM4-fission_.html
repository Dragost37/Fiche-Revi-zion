<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Multimodal fission</h3>

<div class="r-stretch">
<p style="text-decoration: underline;">Role</p>

<ul style="font-size: 25pt;">
<li>Content selection and structuring: select and structure content to be presented</li>
<li>Modality selection: output to be realized with each modality</li>
<li>Output coordination: coordinating each output channel for coherent presentation</li>
</ul>

</div>

<p style="font-size: 10pt; clear:both; text-align: left;">Duarte, C. (2007). Design and evaluation of adaptive multimoldal systems.</p>
</section>


<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Multimodal fission</h3>

<div class="r-stretch">
<p style="text-decoration: underline;">Content selection</p>
<ul style="font-size: 25pt;">
<li>Schema-based: set of predicates</li>
<li>Plan-based: inspired from AI planning "effect, pre-conditions, constraints"</li>
</ul>

</div>

<p style="font-size: 10pt; clear:both; text-align: left;">Foster, M. E. (2002). State of the art review: Multimodal fission. COMIC project Deliverable, 6(09).</p>
</section>
<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Multimodal fission</h3>

<div class="r-stretch">
<p style="text-decoration: underline;">Modality selection</p>

<p>Taxonomy example (Bernsen 1997)</p>
<ul style="font-size: 25pt;">
<li>linguistic vs non-linguistic</li>
<li>arbitrary vs non-arbitrary: how specific the representation is</li>
<li>analogue vs non-analogue: how closely the representation corresponds to the represented object</li>
<li>static vs dynamic</li>
<li>type of physical modality (graphics, acoustics, or haptics)</li>
</ul>

</div>

<p style="font-size: 10pt; clear:both; text-align: left;">Bernsen, N. O. (1997). Defining a taxonomy of output modalities from an HCI perspective. Computer Standards & Interfaces, 18(6-7), 537-553.</p>
</section>

<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Multimodal fission</h3>

<div class="r-stretch">
<p style="text-decoration: underline;">Modality selection</p>

<div style="min-height: 50px;"></div>

<p style="font-size: 25pt;">A set of facts is <strong>expressible</strong> in a graphical language if that language contains a sentence (a collection of graphical objects) that encodes (1) all of the facts in that set, and (2) only those facts. </p>

</div>

<p style="font-size: 10pt; clear:both; text-align: left;">Foster, M. E. (2002). State of the art review: Multimodal fission. COMIC project Deliverable, 6(09).</p>

<h3>Multimodal fission</h3>

<div class="r-stretch">
<p style="text-decoration: underline;">Referring expressions</p>

<ul>
<li>Multimodal referring expressions: refer to world objects using a combination of two or more modalities, e.g., pointing to, looking at, or highlighting the object that is mentioned in language.</li>
<li>Cross-modal referring expressions: refer, not to world objects, but to presentation objects in other modalities.</li>
</ul>


</div>

<p style="font-size: 10pt; clear:both; text-align: left;">Foster, M. E. (2002). State of the art review: Multimodal fission. COMIC project Deliverable, 6(09).</p>

<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Haptics</h3>
<p>Human receptors</p>
<ul style="font-size: 20pt;">
<li>Cutaneous receptors: mechano-receptors (touch, pressure, texture), nociceptors (pain), and thermo-receptors (hot, cold)</li>
<li>Kinaesthetic receptors: muscle, tendon, joint</li>
</ul>

<img src="imgs/skin.png" width="40%"/>
<p style="font-size: 10pt; text-align: left; clear:both;">Serafin, S., Geronazzo, M., Erkut, C., Nilsson, N. C., & Nordahl, R. (2018). Sonic interactions in virtual reality: State of the art, current challenges, and future directions. IEEE computer graphics and applications, 38(2), 31-43.</p>
</section>

<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Haptics</h3>
<p>Taxonomy of haptic interfaces</p>
<ul>
<li>Handheld: controllers</li>
<li>Wearable: electrotactile feedback, haptic vest, exoskeletons, Peltier modules</li>
<li>Encountered types: haptics on demand through robot arms, drones, etc.</li>
<li>Physical props: props placed in physical space aligned with virtual objects</li>
<li>Mid-air: delivery of ultrasonic vibrations</li>
</ul>
<p style="font-size: 10pt; text-align: left; clear:both;">Serafin, S., Geronazzo, M., Erkut, C., Nilsson, N. C., & Nordahl, R. (2018). Sonic interactions in virtual reality: State of the art, current challenges, and future directions. IEEE computer graphics and applications, 38(2), 31-43.</p>

<p>Challenges in haptic interfaces</p>

<li>Spatial-temporal interfaces e.g., for thermal feedback</li>
<li>Movement and interaction constraints e.g., due to weight of equipment</li>
