<ul>

<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Introduction to XR</h3>
<div class="r-stretch">
<div style="min-height: 100px;"></div>
<p>The mixed reality / extended reality continuum </p>
<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Reality-Virtuality_Continuum.svg/2880px-Reality-Virtuality_Continuum.svg.png" alt="The reality to virtuality continuum. Wikipedia">
</div>
<p style="font-size: 10pt; text-align: left;">Milgram, Paul, et al. "Augmented reality: A class of displays on the reality-virtuality continuum." Telemanipulator and telepresence technologies. Vol. 2351. International Society for Optics and Photonics, 1995.</p>
</section>

<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Introduction to XR</h3>
<div class="r-stretch">
<p style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: left;">Reality systems: have for the purpose to effectively communicate the application content to and from the user in an intuitive way as if the user is interacting with the real world.</p>
<div class="r-stretch">
<div style="float: left; width: 30%;">
<img src="imgs/jerald2009.png" alt="Components in an IVE proposed by Jerald 2009">
<p style="font-size: 15pt;">Components of an Immersive Virtual Environment system</p>
</div>
<div style="float: right; width: 65%; font-size: 20pt;">
<ul>
<li class="fragment">Input: collects data from the user</li>
<li class="fragment">Application: includes non-rendering aspects of the virtual world</li>
<li class="fragment">Rendering: is the transformation of the internal representation into a user experience</li>
<li class="fragment">Display: is the physical representation experienced by the user</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

</div>
<p style="font-size: 10pt; clear:both; text-align: left;">Jerald, J. (2015). The VR book: Human-centered design for virtual reality. Morgan & Claypool.</p>
</section>
<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in fade-out">
<h3>Introduction to XR</h3>
<div class="r-stretch">
<!--div style="min-height: 50px;"></div-->
<p>Virtual reality</p>
<div style="float: left; width: 30%;">
<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/d/d3/Holodeck2.jpg" alt="The holodeck of Star Trek">
<p style="font-size: 20pt;"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UeYHmdrAegw">Star Trek Holodeck</a></p>
</div>
<div style="float: right; width: 65%; font-size: 25pt;">
<ul>
<li class="fragment">Term first coined by Antonin Artaud</li>
<li class="fragment">Fully <span class="emph-red">artificial world</span> in which the user is immersed</li>
<li class="fragment"><span style="font-style: italic;">Hamlet on the Holodeck</span> Janet Murray, 1997 : delicate boundary between the <span class="emph-red"> viewer and the narrative</span>, imagining a future with <span class="emph-red">participatory involvement</span> with different cyberdramas</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<p style="font-size: 10pt; clear:both; text-align: left;">Murray, Janet Horowitz. Hamlet on the holodeck: The future of narrative in cyberspace. MIT press, 2017.</p>
</section>
<section data-auto-animate data-transition="fade-in slide-out">
<h3>Introduction to XR</h3>
<div class="r-stretch">
<!--div style="min-height: 50px;"></div-->
<p>Augmented reality</p>
<div style="float: left; width: 30%;">
<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/72/Louis_Rosenberg_Augmented_Reality_Rig.png" alt="Virtual Fixtures">
<p style="font-size: 20pt;">Louis Rosenberg testing Virtual Fixtures</p>
</div>
<div style="float: right; width: 65%; font-size: 25pt;">
<ul>
<li class="fragment">Computer generated information is injected into the real world</li>
<li class="fragment">Telepresence: The fundamental purpose of a telepresence system is to extend an operator‘s <span class="emph-red">sensory-motor facilities</span> and <span class="emph-red">problem solving abilities</span> to a remote environment</li>
<li class="fragment">Overlaying abstract sensory information on top of a workspace</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<p style="font-size: 10pt; text-align: left; clear:both; vertical-align: bottom;">Rosenberg, Louis B. "Virtual fixtures: Perceptual tools for telerobotic manipulation." Proceedings of IEEE virtual reality annual international symposium. IEEE, 1993.</p>
</section>

<div class="r-stretch">

<p style="text-decoration: underline;">GUI (WIMP) vs. MUI</p>
<table>
<tr>
<th style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">GUI (WIMP)</th>
<th style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">MUI</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Single input stream</td>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Multiple input streams</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Atomic, deterministic</td>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Continuous, probabilistic</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Sequential processing</td>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Parallel processing</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Centralized architecture</td>
<td style="font-size: 20pt; text-align: center">Distributed, time-sensitive architectures</td>
</tr>
</table>
